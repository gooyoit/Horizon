## [NASA Discovers Ultra-Rare Neutron Star Merger in Tiny Galaxy](https://www.ithome.com/0/928/992.htm) ⭐️ 9.0/10

NASA telescopes observed an ultra-rare neutron star collision—designated GRB 230906A—in a faint, small galaxy embedded within a vast intergalactic gas stream 4.7 billion light-years away, a location never before seen for such events. This discovery may solve two long-standing astrophysical puzzles: why some short gamma-ray bursts appear far from galactic centers or without visible host galaxies, and how heavy elements like gold and platinum end up in distant stellar regions. It also suggests that galaxy mergers can trigger chains of events leading to neutron star collisions in unexpected locations. The event was pinpointed using NASA’s Fermi, Swift, Chandra, and Hubble telescopes; Chandra’s precise X-ray localization was crucial to identifying the faint host galaxy. The merger likely occurred in a dwarf galaxy formed during a starburst triggered by an ancient galaxy collision about 700 million years ago.

rss · IT HOME · Mar 14, 00:13

**Background**: Neutron star mergers are known to produce short gamma-ray bursts (lasting less than 2 seconds) and are key sites for the rapid neutron-capture process (r-process), which creates elements heavier than iron, such as gold and platinum. The 2017 event GW170817 provided the first direct evidence linking neutron star collisions to both gravitational waves and heavy element production. However, most observed mergers have occurred in larger galaxies, making this discovery in a tiny, isolated system highly unusual.
