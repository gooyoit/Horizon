## [Tinybox Claims Local 120B-Parameter AI Inference](https://tinygrad.org/#tinybox) ⭐️ 8.0/10

Tinybox, an offline AI device developed by tinygrad, claims to run a 120-billion-parameter large language model locally using consumer-grade hardware configurations like six AMD 7900XTX or four RTX 5090 GPUs. The announcement has triggered technical debate over whether such hardware can feasibly support 120B models without extreme quantization or severe context limitations. If feasible, Tinybox could democratize access to powerful AI by enabling decentralized, offline inference without reliance on cloud providers or proprietary APIs. However, skepticism about its real-world performance highlights the ongoing tension between ambitious edge-AI claims and practical hardware constraints. The Red V2 configuration uses six AMD Radeon RX 7900 XTX GPUs (each with 24GB VRAM, totaling 144GB), but community experts note that even with heavy quantization (e.g., 4-bit), running a 120B model leaves little room for key-value (KV) cache, likely limiting context to under 4K tokens and risking degraded output quality.

hackernews · albelfio · Mar 21, 20:08

**Background**: Running large language models (LLMs) locally requires sufficient GPU memory to store model weights, activations, and the key-value cache during inference. A 120B-parameter model in FP16 format needs about 240GB of VRAM, but quantization (e.g., to 4-bit) can reduce this to ~60GB. However, aggressive quantization often degrades model coherence and reasoning ability. Current high-end consumer GPUs typically offer 24–48GB VRAM each, making multi-GPU setups necessary for such large models.
