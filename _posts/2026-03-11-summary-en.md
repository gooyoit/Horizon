## [Computer Science Pioneer Tony Hoare Dies at 92](https://blog.computationalcomplexity.org/2026/03/tony-hoare-1934-2026.html) ⭐️ 9.0/10

Sir Tony Hoare, renowned British computer scientist and inventor of Quicksort, null references, and Communicating Sequential Processes (CSP), has died in 2026 at the age of 92. His passing has sparked widespread tributes from the global computing community. Hoare’s work laid foundational pillars for modern programming languages, formal verification, and concurrent systems. His ideas continue to influence software correctness, language design, and system reliability across academia and industry. Among his most impactful contributions are Quicksort (a widely used sorting algorithm), Hoare logic (a formal system for reasoning about program correctness), and CSP (a formal language for describing concurrency). He also famously called the invention of the null reference his 'billion-dollar mistake.'

**Background**: Tony Hoare, also known as C.A.R. Hoare, was a British computer scientist who received the Turing Award in 1980 for his fundamental contributions to programming language design. His work on formal methods—mathematically rigorous techniques for specifying and verifying software—helped establish the field of program correctness. Concepts like CSP influenced later concurrency models in languages such as Go and Erlang.

## [Yann LeCun Raises $1B for AI with Physical World Understanding](https://www.wired.com/story/yann-lecun-raises-dollar1-billion-to-build-ai-that-understands-the-physical-world/) ⭐️ 9.0/10

Yann LeCun has secured $1 billion in funding to build a startup focused on developing AI systems that understand the physical world through grounded, spatiotemporal world models rather than relying solely on textual data. This initiative represents a potential paradigm shift away from current large language models (LLMs), which lack true understanding of physical reality, toward AI that can reason about space, time, and causality—critical for robotics, autonomous systems, and artificial general intelligence (AGI). The new company, reportedly named AMI (Amilabs), is not an applied AI firm but focuses on foundational research; it aims to build world models trained on multimodal sensory data like video, not just language. hackernews · helloplanets · Mar 10, 08:46

**Background**: World models are internal representations that allow AI systems to simulate and predict how objects and environments behave over time and space. Unlike LLMs—which learn statistical patterns from text—world models aim to capture the dynamics of the physical world, enabling reasoning about cause-and-effect, object permanence, and motion. Spatiotemporal models process data that varies across both space and time, such as video or sensor streams, making them suitable for real-world interaction. Grounded AI refers to systems whose knowledge is tied to observable reality rather than abstract linguistic correlations.

## [Google Launches Gemini Embedding 2, a Natively Multimodal Model](https://blog.google/innovation-and-ai/models-and-research/gemini-models/gemini-embedding-2/) ⭐️ 9.0/10

Google has released Gemini Embedding 2 in public preview via the Gemini API and Vertex AI. It is Google’s first natively multimodal embedding model that maps text, images, video, audio, and documents into a unified vector space. This model significantly advances retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) and semantic search by enabling cross-modal understanding and long-context inputs, allowing developers to build more powerful and context-aware AI applications. Its native multimodal support eliminates the need for separate encoders per modality, streamlining development and improving semantic alignment. Gemini Embedding 2 supports up to 8192 tokens per input, processes up to 6 images or 120 seconds of video, embeds PDFs up to 6 pages, and outputs 3072-dimensional vectors that can be reduced on demand. It integrates with popular frameworks like LangChain for RAG pipelines.

telegram · zaihuapd · Mar 10, 16:52

**Background**: Embedding models convert data—such as text, images, or audio—into numerical vectors that capture semantic meaning, enabling similarity search and retrieval in AI systems. Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) enhances large language models by fetching relevant external information using these embeddings before generating responses. Multimodal embedding models aim to represent different data types in a shared vector space so that, for example, a text query can retrieve relevant images or videos. Google’s Gemini family, introduced in December 2023, includes large multimodal models designed to handle diverse input types natively.
