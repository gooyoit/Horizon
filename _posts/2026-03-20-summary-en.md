## [KittenML Releases Three Tiny, Expressive Open-Source TTS Models](https://github.com/KittenML/KittenTTS) ⭐️ 7.0/10

KittenML released three new open-source text-to-speech (TTS) models with 14M, 40M, and 80M parameters, the smallest of which is under 25MB and achieves state-of-the-art (SOTA) expressivity for its size class. The models support eight expressive English voices (four male, four female) and are optimized for on-device use via ONNX and int8/fp16 quantization. This release significantly advances on-device generative audio by offering high-quality, expressive speech synthesis in extremely compact models that run without a GPU—enabling voice applications on low-power devices like Raspberry Pi, wearables, and smartphones. It helps close the gap between cloud-based and local TTS, promoting privacy, accessibility, and offline functionality. The models are quantized to int8 + fp16 and use ONNX runtime for cross-platform compatibility; they output audio at 24 kHz and include voices named Bella, Jasper, Luna, Bruno, Rosie, Hugo, Kiki, and Leo. Despite their small size, they achieve strong expressivity, though some users note issues with number pronunciation and unnecessary CUDA dependencies during installation.

hackernews · rohan_joshi · Mar 19, 15:56

**Background**: Text-to-speech (TTS) systems convert written text into spoken audio. Traditional high-quality TTS models are large and require cloud inference, but recent efforts focus on efficient, on-device models for privacy and latency reasons. 'Expressivity' in TTS refers to the model’s ability to convey natural prosody, emotion, and intonation—typically challenging in small models. ONNX is an open format for deploying machine learning models across different hardware and software platforms.
